Computer Science Visualizer

Home Djungle Maze Plant Purpose Quick-Lane Ignitus Land N' Deed CS Visualizer

Computer Science is an amazing topic that covers a vaste variety of ideas and concepts. However, learning these abstract lines of thought can be incredibly challenging depending on one's learning style. So, I wanted to make these tough topics more visual and digestable to people not as used to learning mental concepts.

Made for computer science students, this visualizer, which will continue to be expanded upon to help me re enforce my own learning, covers sorting aglorithms and data structures that can begin taking junior developers to the next level. See merge sort, selection sort, bubble sort, and insertion sort in action with a dataset of 100 randomly generated, shuffled numbers. Then you can visualize some data structures as you build your own linked list, doubly linked list, stack, or queue and it'll appear on screen showing the connections and values of the structure.
